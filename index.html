Member Guidelines:
Some general guidelines for reasonable expectations of conduct and operations among members of the commons are as follow:

  • Within the Canto Commons, members are encouraged to be autonomous and self-directed in the research, discovery, and execution of high-value initiatives.
  • Allow deliberate experimentation regarding the implementation of a specification; encourage creativity, but be stringent in meeting the deadlines everyone has agreed upon.
  • Be concise and deliberate in the delivery of your communications, whether via text, code, or speech.
  • Maintain a healthy disdain toward hierarchies, but hold in high regard the roles and contributions of your code companions.
  • Within reason, it is acceptable to move as fast as possible while maintaining adequate safety and security standards in the delivery of public goods within the Canto ecosystem.
Members of the Commons are expected to behave cordially to one another. Aggressive behavior will not be tolerated.

Roles:
Everyone has a role in the Canto Commons. This role does not provide explicit power, but implicit power.

Everyone plays a fluid role, meaning that one may be better suited for the responsibility of managing the implementation of a protocol, but may not be the best person to get the job done within the context of a separate, albeit similar, project.

An individual will be elected from the Commons in order to act as primary Project Manager. It will be their responsibility to make good faith decisions in order to ensure that the project is complete and in good order for public presentation.
